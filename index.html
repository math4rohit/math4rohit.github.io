About Me

I am an applied cryptographer with a Ph.D. in mathematics, focused on the formal design and security analysis of privacy-preserving protocols (FHE, MPC, ZKPs) and post-quantum algorithms (PQC). At QNu Labs, within the Center of Excellence in Post-Quantum Cryptography, I work at the intersection of theory and engineering—developing high-assurance implementations, hardening primitives against side-channel leakage, and improving protocol efficiency through rigorous modeling.

I completed my Ph.D. at MNNIT Allahabad (Prayagraj) (2022–2025), and was selected as one of three global laureates for the Post-Quantum Algebraic Cryptography trimester at the Institut Henri Poincaré, Paris. I previously taught as Assistant Professor of Mathematics at Vaze College, Mumbai (2019–2022). Together, research, industry, and teaching shape my work on practical, post-quantum cryptographic systems.
