About Me

I hold a Ph.D. in cryptography, specializing in the formal design and security analysis of privacy-preserving protocols (FHE, MPC, ZKPs) and post-quantum algorithms (PQC). As an Applied Cryptographer at QNu Labs within the Center of Excellence in Post-Quantum Cryptography, I bridge mathematical foundations with high-assurance implementations—hardening primitives against side-channel leakages and optimizing protocol efficiency through rigorous modeling.

Previously, I completed my Ph.D. at MNNIT Allahabad (Prayagraj) as Junior and Senior Research Fellow, and was a Laureate for the Post-Quantum Algebraic Cryptography trimester at the Institut Henri Poincaré in Paris, and an Assistant Professor of Mathematics at Vaze College, Mumbai. This diverse background in both academic research and teaching informs my current work on practical, next-generation cryptographic solutions.

My core areas of expertise include:

  • Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE): Enables computations directly on encrypted data without decryption, revolutionizing privacy-preserving data processing in domains such as cloud computing and secure analytics.
  • Multi-Party Computation (MPC): Facilitates collaborative computation among multiple parties while keeping individual inputs confidential, ensuring data privacy in distributed and adversarial environments.
  • Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs): Allows one party to prove the validity of a statement without revealing any underlying data, critical for secure authentication and privacy in blockchain and identity systems.
